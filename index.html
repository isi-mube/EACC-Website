Welcome to the European Advisory Committee of Cytotechnology website!

In Europe, there are about 4000 Cytotechnologists. Without their constant dedicated work, the fight against cervical cancer would be impossible. With this website, we aim to enhance communication between cytotechnological delegates from all European countries under the auspices of the EFCS. .

Goals of the EACC

  • To serve as a forum for discussion on cytotechnological education and professional issues in Europe.
  • To formulate guidelines and recommendations aimed at improving cytotechnology standards and harmonizing practices in Europe.
  • To provide advice and counsel to the European Federation of Cytology Societies (EFCS).
  • For more information, please check the EACC Regulations.

The European Advisory Committee of Cytotechnology (EACC)

The EACC Advisory Committee is a body composed of cytotechnologists from different European countries, reporting to the EFCS. The advisory committee addresses educational and professional issues relating to cytotechnologists in Europe, working to improve standards in the profession.

REGULATIONS

OF THE EUROPEAN ADVISORY COMMITTEE OF CYTOTECHNOLOGY (EACC) OF THE EUROPEAN FEDERATION OF CYTOLOGY SOCIETIES (EFCS)

The EACC’s aims and activities include all subjects concerning the Cytotechnologist (CT) profession.

The committee is composed of Members, Chairperson, and Secretary.

EACC is responsible for the European Special Session for Cytotechnologists to be organized and held during the European Congress of Cytology in co-operation with the host country.

The Annual EACC meeting takes place at every European Congress of Cytology.

At least half of the members must be present or represented at the meeting in order to make voting, decisions, or changes. Members, Chairperson, and Secretary have a right to vote.

A non-member can attend the meeting only if approval is given by the Chairperson and the Secretary of EACC. If the Member is represented by a non-member, that person does not have the right to vote.

Decisions are taken by a simple majority of the votes. In the event of an equality of votes, the Chairperson or the one who chairs the meeting will have the casting vote.

Membership

Membership in the EACC is by National Society, not by individual. Members are nominated to sit in the EACC by the National Cytotechnology/Cytology Society in their home country; preferably one per European country. If more than one National Society exists in the country, it is possible that each Society has a member in EACC.

Request for Admission

A request for admission of a National Cytotechnology/Cytology Society representative for membership shall be submitted to the Chairperson and Secretary of the EACC, accompanied with a recommendation letter from the Chairperson or Secretary of the National Cytotechnology/Cytology Society that contains the following personal data of the proposed member/representative:

  • Name, surname, institution, and address of employment
  • Contact telephone number, e-mail address
  • Full name and website of the National Society which the mentioned person represents

An EACC member should meet the following requirements:

  • Must be a member and elected representative of the National Cytotechnology/Cytology Society represented in the EACC
  • Should preferably serve for at least 4 years with the possibility of extending the membership
  • A retiring member is succeeded by a Cytotechnologist elected in his/her country
  • Live and work in a country that the member represents
  • Be reasonably fluent in written and oral English
  • Be a highly qualified Cytotechnologist with experience in the Cytotechnology profession
  • Be familiar with the national situation related to Cytotechnology, e.g. education, certification and status, tasks and responsibilities, salaries, continuing education, etc.
  • Should have experience at a managerial level (i.e. chief CT, member of a professional board, teacher…)
  • Be in a position to attend European Cytology Congresses, participate at the Annual EACC meeting and the Special Session for Cytotechnologists
  • To actively participate in the Special Session for Cytotechnologists
  • To suggest subjects and actively participate in discussions at the EACC meetings
  • To invite Cytotechnologists from his/her own country to actively contribute to the Special Session for Cytotechnologists
  • Notify the Secretary on any changes in personal and National Society data

Chairperson of the EACC

The Chairperson must have been a Secretary of the EACC for at least one mandate before carrying out the mandate of Chairperson. The Chairperson should preferably serve for at least 4 years, with the possibility of re-election.

Responsibilities:

  • With commitment and active work, contribute to the reputation, position, and progress of EACC
  • Officially represent EACC, cooperate with other professional organizations/societies together with the EACC Secretary on behalf of the EACC
  • Chair the Annual EACC Meeting (in case of inability to attend, the Secretary chairs)
  • Suggest, prepare, and approve the Agenda for the EACC Meeting together with the EACC Secretary
  • Invite the Secretary General of the EFCS to the Annual Meeting, together with the Secretary of the EACC
  • Chair the Special Session for Cytotechnologists at the European Congress of Cytology and choose/invite a co-chair
  • Arrange, invite, and select lecturers for the Special Session for Cytotechnologists together with the Secretary
  • Approve all EACC documents and material for publication on the EACC website
  • Approve requests for membership of new members in the EACC together with the Secretary
  • Request renewal for Advisory Member position in the EFCS together with the newly elected Secretary

Secretary of the EACC

The Secretary must be a member of the EACC for at least 4 years and elected by the EACC members. The Secretary should preferably serve for 4 years with the possibility of re-election. When the Chairperson retires, the Secretary automatically replaces the Chairperson.

Responsibilities:

  • With commitment and active work, contribute to the reputation, position, and progress of EACC
  • Officially represent EACC together with the Chairperson and cooperate with other professional organizations/societies on behalf of the EACC
  • Consider and approve requests for membership of new members together with the Chairperson
  • Update the EACC member list and take care of EACC documents
  • Organize the venue, time, and other needs for the Annual Meeting
  • Collaborate with the Chairperson to draw up and distribute the Agenda for the Annual EACC Meeting
  • Send the Minutes of the meeting to the EACC members within 2 months after approval by the Chairperson
  • Send a copy of the Minutes to the Secretary of the EFCS
  • Organize the Special Session for Cytotechnologists at the European Congress of Cytology together with the Chairperson and EACC members
  • Request renewal for Advisory Member position in the EFCS together with the newly elected Chairperson

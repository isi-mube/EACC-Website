Welcome to the European Advisory Committee of Cytotechnology website!

In Europe, there are about 4000 Cytotechnologists. Without their constant dedicated work, the fight against cervical cancer would be impossible.

With this web-based communication tool, we aim to enhance communication between cytotechnological delegates from all European countries under the auspices of the EFCS. .

Goals of the EACC

  • To serve as a forum for discussion on cytotechnological education and professional issues in Europe.
  • To formulate guidelines and recommendations aimed at improving cytotechnology standards and harmonizing practices in Europe.
  • To provide advice and counsel to the European Federation of Cytology Societies (EFCS).
  • For more information, please check the EACC Regulations.

The European Advisory Committee of Cytotechnology (EACC)

The EACC Advisory Committee is a body composed of cytotechnologists from different European countries, reporting to the EFCS. The advisory committee addresses educational and professional issues relating to cytotechnologists in Europe, working to improve standards in the profession.
